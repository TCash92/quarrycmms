# Downtime Tracking Form (Baseline Period)

> **Purpose**: Track all unplanned equipment downtime during the 2-week baseline period BEFORE launching QuarryCMMS. This data will be compared against post-launch metrics to calculate ROI.

---

## Instructions

1. **Complete one form per asset per week**
2. **Record EVERY unplanned downtime event** (not scheduled maintenance)
3. **Fill out immediately** when equipment goes down - don't wait until end of shift
4. **Submit to supervisor** at end of each week

## Cause Definitions

| Cause | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Wore out** | Gradual degradation - belts worn thin, bearings getting noisy, filters clogged over time |
| **Broke** | Sudden unexpected failure - shaft snapped, motor burned out, hydraulic line burst |
| **Unknown** | Can't determine the cause (but still record the event!) |
| **Other** | Anything else - write a brief description |

---

## Tips for Accurate Recording

1. **Don't round times** - Record actual times, not "about 10am"
2. **Include travel time** - If you drove to get parts, that's downtime
3. **Be specific** - "Replaced belt" is better than "Fixed it"
4. **If equipment is still down at end of shift** - Leave "Up at" blank, complete next day
5. **Multiple failures same day** - Each gets its own event entry

---

## What Counts as Downtime?

**YES - Record these:**
- Equipment won't start
- Emergency stops due to failure
- Running at reduced capacity due to breakdown
- Waiting for parts after failure

**NO - Don't record these:**
- Scheduled maintenance (PM)
- Shift changes
- Weather delays
- Lunch breaks
- Operational decisions (choosing not to run)
